Rock & Iteration

Rock & Iteration 15/08/2013

“Come on, i can’t believe there is any relationship with rock music and Iteration.”

“Is there any connection between these things? If there is,what is the connection?”

“Why rock music,what about the classic?”

Perhaps you will have a similar idea after reading the title, but in fact, they can be combined together.

Xiang YiNing, which was a designers and musician, has dished out the “music is product” concept for the first time in an event organized by Geek Park last year , he said:

“A product is a song, the starting point of one rock song is a piece of RIFF, of a product is an idea. Band leader equivalent to the CEO, creative soul of a band as product manager, lead singer / guitarist as visual designer, bass player as interaction designer and drummer as UED / programmer.”

He put music as Internet products. However, the relationship between music and Internet products actually more than that, meanwhile,”iteration” and music can be associated together.

To those product manager, this is not a new word, and “Quick on-line, rapid iteration” is one of the basic methods of product development.

Associated how?

Step 1:Find the key points.

The key to the Internet products’ iteration is to collect product data,which is user’s feedback data, then summarize, analyze and identify further updated version. Similarly, the key to the rock music is also collecting feedback data, precisely,which is the audience response.

But the question is, how to collect data in the music scene of the performance? No matter where the band members performed ,such as some poor light mini Live House (like the Foolish Old Man and MAO Livehouse), some bars, or even in a large music festival, it is very difficult to meter audience’ reaction, because:

1.The feeling slipped away;

2.You can’t get the answer by look at a person’s face, maybe this guy is Steven Seagal.

Geeks Park recently met a bunch of people who claimed that they can solve this problem, they call themself Sonidigg. This group used a reasonable way to collect data in order to determine the audience response.

Step 2:Get the data right, get the right data.

Let’s get back to the beginning of July MIIC Mobile Internet Innovation Conference, JooMe installed an router and WiFi positioning sensors in the audience’ head which capture the audience networked data, after analyzed these data they successfully quantify the popularity of a speech.

Sonidigg has a similar way to finish this kind of data collection, but the focus shifted from the head to the feet, and the acquisition devices are also turned into a rug from the router, they called it Pedimoni open source carpets.

The carpet made by Sonidigg’ founder Balloon Wang, he is also the lead singer of the band named Churen Huai Yu Wang. As a musician, he also encountered the problem mentioned at the beginning, “Came to light after a fight, following a dark, watching less than the audience, the sound i heard and the voice made by audience is not the same, nobody can determine the performance effects. “So he decided to do something to help him out to determine the effect of live performances, the band then be arranged according to this thing adjusted.

In his opinion, you can titillate cheers in a rock show, applause can beg, but the audience’s response was not really hidden. For us humans, the most instinctive understanding of music begins with one step, it can be said that the audience of the “lower body” is an important criterion for detection of live performances. So Wang and his team put out a carpet, which uses piezoelectric ceramic to perceive the deformation on the floor, collecting, aggregating, and analyzing all the signal, but because the carpet is not easy to deploy, so follow the evolution of both the installation or even more simple and convenient removal of carpet.

By observing these data, we can find out when synchronized came out, there is a jump or some fancy Solo, its effect POGO gain much more than we expect. They can tell you that which song or even what part of this popular glance is really “popular”. Bands can check the data reporting, analysis, which tracks exactly which part needs to be improved, and thus the formation of new rock melodies.

POGO Thermometer

POGO Thermometer 16/12/2012

Bolloon Wang will share some idea about big data in music field on this show.

Some people are already exploring the power of data in realms outside of health. Wang Bolong, the lead singer of punk band Bear Warrior, wanted to find a way to measure the audience response in order to see how well his band really performed.

“If you ask a fan what he or she thinks of the show, they only give you vague feedback like ‘it was good,’ but nothing specific that we could actually use to improve ourselves,” said Wang, whose stage name is “Mr Pregnant.” The 24-year-old is also a graduate student majoring in precision instruments at Beijing University of Aeronautics and Astronautics.

In September he set out to design a device called the “POGO Thermometer,” which measures the intensity of the audience’s dancing.

After three months of trial and error, he embedded several sensors in a carpet that transmit data on people’s movements to a computer where it is analyzed. The data is then transformed into graphs to give a clear visual presentation of the audience’s response.

Based on his examination of the results from one performance, Wang found that fans started moving their bodies when the drums kicked in, and they danced the most energetically when he sang higher notes.

Wang believes that such data helps the band improve its songwriting, arrangements and performance. He concluded after analyzing the data for one of their songs that the chorus was too monotonous. The audience seemed bored, and they did not respond with much enthusiasm, prompting Wang to consider working on the band’s stage presence.

Such data tells performers whether they have delivered the emotion and meaning they wanted to convey through their music, explained Wang. “The data helps us understand how we can improve our performance to make the audience respond to our music like we intend,” he said.

The carpet debuted in the band’s performance at Mao Livehouse on December 6. Since then he has collected data from more than 10 concerts, including over 20 bands, and has recorded the impact of dancing feet during over 120 songs. Soon he plans to make the data public in order to share findings and adopt better analysis tools.
